"Being skilled at something and wanting to do it every day are not the same thing."

Most career tools ask what you're good at, then recommend jobs that use those skills. This sounds logical. It's the reason so many lawyers end up in roles that look different on paper and feel exactly the same.

The session you're about to start treats your skills and your preferred daily work as two completely separate questions. It will never recommend a role that sends you back into what you're done with — even if you're excellent at it.

Your legal background will get you hired. What fills your days will be built around something else entirely.

Sit with these for 60 seconds before you continue
1 What are you good at that you're done with?
2 What do you actually like doing — even buried inside a job you hate?

You don't need to answer out loud. The session will draw it out. Having these in mind will make your answers sharper.
